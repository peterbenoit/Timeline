NNDSS Timeline

  1. 2020

    CDC publishes the first of a series of technical guidance documents, data standards, and implementation guides, including guides for Electronic Laboratory Reporting (ELR), Case Notification, and Vital Records Death Reporting.

  2. 2017

    CDC begins onboarding jurisdictions to send case notification data using HL7 message mapping guides. By the end of the year, 10 jurisdictions are routinely sending production data for at least one condition.

  3. 2014

    CDC launches the NNDSS Modernization Initiative (NMI) as part of the CDC Surveillance Strategy. Through this multi-year initiative, CDC increased the robustness of the NNDSS technological infrastructure with interoperable, standardized data and exchange mechanisms.

  4. 2010

    An updated case definition for dengue, a nationally notifiable condition, is approved by CSTE. CDC and CSTE implement the use of electronic case notification processing procedures for all nationally notifiable diseases.

  5. 2007

    Council of State and Territorial Epidemiologists (CSTE) approves a position statement that supports implementation of International Health Regulations in the U.S. This international legal instrument governs information sharing about events that might constitute a Public Health Emergency of International Concern.

  6. 2000

    CDC creates the National Electronic Telecommunications System for Surveillance (NETSS), which provides computerized transmission of notifiable disease data from states to CDC. This system marks a significant improvement in the way health data is reported and analyzed.
